Unlocking the Secrets of Mars

Since 1997, NASA has deployed a fleet of robotic vehicles to traverse the dusty, crater-filled terrain of Mars. These remarkable machines serve as our eyes, ears, and hands on a world millions of miles away.

Each rover carries sophisticated scientific instruments designed to analyze rocks, study the atmosphere, and search for evidence that Mars may have once supported microbial life. Their discoveries have transformed our understanding of the fourth planet from the Sun.

Explore this site to learn about the incredible engineering behind these missions, the groundbreaking science they've accomplished, and what lies ahead for Mars exploration.

Rovers

Perseverance

  • Landed in February 2021, Perseverance is the most advanced rover ever sent to Mars.
  • It carries the Ingenuity helicopter and is collecting samples for a future return mission to Earth.

Curiosity

  • Operating since August 2012 in Gale Crater on Mars.
  • First rover to determine that Mars once had conditions favorable for microbial life.

Spirit

  • One of the twin Mars Exploration Rovers, active from 2004 to 2010.
  • Far exceeded its planned 90-day mission on the Martian surface.

Background

The NASA Mars rover program is a pioneering effort to explore the Red Planet using robotic vehicles designed to traverse its surface and conduct scientific experiments. Beginning with Sojourner in 1997, the program has since launched a series of increasingly advanced rovers, including Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.
