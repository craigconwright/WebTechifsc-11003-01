Mission History

NASA's Mars Exploration Program has sent multiple robotic explorers to the Red Planet. Each mission has built upon previous discoveries, pushing the boundaries of what we know about Mars and paving the way for future human exploration.

All Mars Rovers

Perseverance

  • Launch Date: July 30, 2020
  • Landing Date: February 18, 2021
  • Landing Site: Jezero Crater
  • Status: Active
  • Perseverance is searching for signs of ancient microbial life and collecting rock samples for future return to Earth.

Curiosity

  • Launch Date: November 26, 2011
  • Landing Date: August 6, 2012
  • Landing Site: Gale Crater
  • Status: Active
  • Curiosity discovered that Mars once had conditions favorable for microbial life.

Spirit

  • Launch Date: June 10, 2003
  • Landing Date: January 4, 2004
  • Landing Site: Gusev Crater
  • Status: Mission Complete (2010)
  • Spirit found evidence that Mars once had hot springs and volcanic activity.

Earlier Missions

Opportunity (2004-2018)

Spirit's twin rover, Opportunity landed on the opposite side of Mars. It held the record for longest distance driven by any off-Earth vehicle, traveling over 28 miles during its 14-year mission.

Sojourner (1997)

The first rover to operate on Mars, Sojourner was part of the Mars Pathfinder mission. Though small (about the size of a microwave oven), it proved that roving vehicles could explore the Martian surface.

Mission Goals

Each Mars rover mission has specific scientific goals, but they all share common objectives: understanding Mars' climate and geology, determining whether Mars ever supported life, and preparing for future human exploration of the Red Planet.
