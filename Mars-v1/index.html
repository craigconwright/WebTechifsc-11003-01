Unlocking the Secrets of Mars

Since 1997, NASA has deployed a fleet of robotic vehicles to traverse the dusty, crater-filled terrain of Mars. These remarkable machines serve as our eyes, ears, and hands on a world millions of miles away.

Each rover carries sophisticated scientific instruments designed to analyze rocks, study the atmosphere, and search for evidence that Mars may have once supported microbial life. Their discoveries have transformed our understanding of the fourth planet from the Sun.

Explore this site to learn about the incredible engineering behind these missions, the groundbreaking science they've accomplished, and what lies ahead for Mars exploration.

Meet the Rovers

Perseverance

Landed in February 2021, Perseverance is the most advanced rover ever sent to Mars. It carries the Ingenuity helicopter and is collecting samples for a future return mission to Earth.

Location: Jezero Crater, an ancient river delta believed to have once held water.

Curiosity

Operating since August 2012, Curiosity was the first rover to determine that Mars once had conditions favorable for microbial life. It continues exploring Gale Crater today.

Key finding: Discovered evidence of an ancient streambed where water once flowed.

Spirit

One of the twin Mars Exploration Rovers, Spirit operated from 2004 to 2010, far exceeding its planned 90-day mission. It found compelling evidence of past water activity.

Legacy: Traveled over 4.8 miles across the Martian surface during its mission.
